Exercises searching tool

Product Design, UI, UX, Research
Brazilian teachers have 12 hours per day work routines: it doesn't end in the classrooms and goes home with them, occupying their personal lives. The Questions Searcher was created to give their time back.

The beginning

In December 2019 I was invited to the first B2B team of Descomplica, to work with schools. We started facing the challenge of the fresh business model and a new universe of problems and opportunities making teacher's lives better. After all, they are who choose which pedagogic assets integrate the school and the classroom.

Our problem and opportunity

Teachers take their work home and don't have time for themselves. We decided to attack this issue by reducing the effort of creating exams and training material for students: a task that involves a long Google search.

We had two months to develop the product, investigate the solution value to the users and, through this, observe how the teacher would see the Descomplica.

Benchmark and scope construction

I started the project analyzing the most relevant questions search tool on the marketing, the SuperPro. Beyond dissecting its architecture I asked teachers that already used SuperPro to explain to me how the product works. With this, I mapped their mental model when creating exams and training lists.

After mapping, I started taking to the paper the central interactions.

Wireframing the problem to visualize the main interactions

Our product would have five main interactions:

  • Search by topic;
  • Filtering and selecting;
  • Selection edditing;
  • Save and share digital exercise list;
  • Visualize answers

We didn't have much time, so I decided to align the scope with high-fidelity wireframes.

The challenges of the experience

Navigation in our pedagogic system

What happens if that user searches for a therm different than the Descomplica's pedagogic system? To prevent confusion, the search tool also has navigation by discipline.

From left to right: high-fidelity wireframes and the result - search links and discipline navigation.
Filtering for questions

The Benchmark and some user interviews showed us the clear necessity of a robust filtering experience. Each discipline, classroom, and school demanded specific approaches in the tasks. After some experiment UI experiments and heuristic evaluation, we decided on an always visible filtering feature.

From left to right: wireframes with possible filter interfaces and the final choice.
Sharing and applying

The Searcher itself is a simple product, but the offline exercises application flow is a delicate point. We're instructing them in detail because we know that an unfocused classroom can be bad for our product.

We started with a possible saving modal, but we're trying to padronize interactions like this in fullscreen. Also considered a page tho edit and save in the same moment, but the flows where different enough to be separated.

Discoveries and the future

With the user interviews that I conducted during the development phase, we discovered that the absence of interest in bringing technology to the classroom may be a myth. We have the hypothesis that the time to discover and learn about digital tools is the real problem. In this scenario, I'm working in an experiment to empower teachers to use Descomplica tools: the Descomplica Methodologies.
