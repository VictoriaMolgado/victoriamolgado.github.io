Descomplica Exercise tools

Product Design, UI, UX, Research, Concept
In May 2018 I and my small startup-team were acquired by Descomplica, one of the biggest edtechs in Brazil. Our mission was extinguishing the exercise features deficits of the online classes platform. In one year we delivered three products: Exercises tool, Exams, and Essays.

Context

We worked with two needs of Brazilians studying to get on a university: training and diagnostics.

Diagnostics: The exams experience

It was our first release by the name of the company: an open and free tool for simulating entrance exams and evaluate their performances.

Exercises tool: The training experience

Someone who's preparing for entrance exams should learn the exam's characteristics and answering timing. That's why we create a tool with exercise lists per subject - with a maximum of 10 questions, a shorter feedback cycle than students are used to and giving the sensation of productivity.

Essays: A strategic skill for students

Our last product created together was a tool for students to write and send essays to be evaluated by a pedagogic team of correctors. It was a new experience to replace an embedded service that adapts itself to receive many corrections styles and communicating just one of them for the student.

The students

In a universe of 200 thousand users, we had a lot of user profiles. Our User Studies team synthesis them in six personas. In the Exercises team, we focused on two of them - by biases they where the public with training time in their routine: students who use Descomplica as a periodic study platform, students that watched live classes every day.

The Exercises feature path - our hypothesis

One year learning

With the product in the market in three months of conception and implementation, I had one year to test it with users. We also had a fixed feedback field on the main page, and that how I discovered that our proposition wasn't valuable for a significant number of users.

In this scenario, while my team worked to develop and ship the Essays feature I started an internal personal project to rethink the exercises experience to be more generic to users cases.
